# Handling High-Stakes One-Hour Resolution Commitment as an Engineering Manager

When senior leadership commits to resolving an issue within a very tight timeframe (e.g., one hour), failing to meet that commitment can risk losing customer trust and damage leadership’s confidence. In such high-stakes situations, the approach must be strategic, focusing on managing expectations and maximizing impact within the limited time.

---

## Steps to Handle the Situation

### 1. Immediately Set Realistic Expectations
- Clarify with leadership what “resolution” means — full fix, workaround, or mitigation.
- If a full fix is unlikely within one hour, propose delivering a **workaround or mitigation** first.
- Communicate this clearly to leadership and, if appropriate, to the customer.

### 2. Rapid Triage and Prioritization
- Quickly identify the **root cause or highest impact area**.
- Focus efforts on the **highest-impact fix or workaround** achievable within the hour.
- Avoid distractions by less critical issues.

### 3. Mobilize a “Tiger Team”
- Assemble your best experts immediately.
- Assign clear roles: diagnosis, fix implementation, and communication.

### 4. Transparent and Frequent Communication
- Provide updates every 10-15 minutes to leadership and customer stakeholders.
- Communicate blockers and next steps honestly if progress is slow.
- Emphasize efforts to minimize impact and plans beyond the one-hour window.

### 5. Deliver What You Can Within the Hour
- Even if a full resolution isn’t possible, deliver a **meaningful mitigation or workaround**.
- This demonstrates progress and commitment, helping maintain trust.

### 6. Plan and Communicate Next Steps
- Outline the plan for full resolution after the one-hour window.
- Provide timelines and assign ownership for follow-up actions.
- Ensure leadership and customer know you are fully committed.

### 7. Post-Incident Review and Relationship Repair
- Conduct a thorough review to identify gaps.
- Share lessons learned and improvements to prevent recurrence.
- Engage proactively with customer and leadership to rebuild trust.

---

## Key Mindset and Communication Tips

- **Be proactive, not reactive:** Keep everyone informed continuously.
- **Focus on impact, not perfection:** Partial progress is better than none.
- **Own the situation:** Take responsibility and show urgency.
- **Show empathy:** Acknowledge customer frustration and leadership pressure.

---
## Summary

As an Engineering Manager, when faced with a one-hour resolution commitment:

- Set realistic expectations upfront.
- Deliver the highest-impact mitigation possible within the timeframe.
- Communicate transparently and frequently.
- Have a clear plan for full resolution immediately afterward.

This approach helps preserve trust even if the ideal outcome isn’t achieved within the hour.
