# What Motivates Me to Become an Engineering Manager

Becoming an Engineering Manager is a natural progression in my career driven by a combination of passion for technology, leadership, and a desire to make a broader impact. Here are the key motivations behind my aspiration:

- Learn from other but build your own way to tackle the situation back yourself
- Go to management with the plan if are slipping but what is the next plan of action and plan for the next plan of action 
---

## Shifting from fixing technical bugs to solving complex business and organizational challenges.
- Finding fulfillment in mentoring others, seeing them succeed, and building a high-performing team culture.
- Stepping up to lead to protect a great team from poor management or to ensure technical decisions remain sound.

## Fundamental Mindset Shifts
- You must learn to delegate and allow others to solve technical problems, even if they do it differently than you would.
- Unlike the immediate feedback of working code, management results (like team growth) can take months to manifest.
- You move from being a "superstar" technical expert to a "beginner" manager, which some describe as an "identity demotion". 


## 1. Passion for Enabling Others to Succeed

- I find great satisfaction in helping engineers grow their skills and careers.
- Mentoring and coaching team members to reach their full potential motivates me deeply.
- Seeing individuals and teams succeed because of my support is highly rewarding.

## 2. Driving Impact Beyond Individual Contribution

- As an individual contributor, my impact is limited to my own work.
- As a manager, I can influence the success of entire teams and projects.
- I am motivated by the opportunity to align engineering efforts with business goals and deliver meaningful value.

## 3. Solving Complex People and Process Challenges

- Engineering management involves navigating team dynamics, communication, and process improvements.
- I enjoy tackling these challenges to create a productive and positive work environment.
- Facilitating collaboration and removing blockers energizes me.

## 4. Continuous Learning and Growth

- The role requires developing new skills in leadership, strategy, and organizational management.
- I am motivated by the opportunity to grow personally and professionally.
- Embracing this challenge keeps me engaged and inspired.

## 5. Building and Shaping High-Performing Teams

- I am passionate about fostering a culture of innovation, accountability, and continuous improvement.
- Creating an environment where creativity and excellence thrive motivates me.
- I want to contribute to building teams that deliver outstanding results.

---

## Summary

My motivation to become an Engineering Manager stems from a desire to empower others, drive broader impact, solve complex challenges, and continuously grow as a leader. I am excited by the opportunity to shape teams and projects that make a difference.
