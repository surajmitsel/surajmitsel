# Performance Improvement Plan (PIP)

## 1. Identify Specific Performance Issues
Clearly document the areas where the employee’s performance is lacking. This should be objective, measurable, and based on facts (e.g., missed deadlines, quality issues, lack of teamwork).

## 2. Set Clear, Measurable Goals
Define what successful performance looks like. For example:
- Complete project reports within 3 days of deadline with less than 2% errors.

## 3. Define a Timeline
Usually, a PIP lasts 30, 60, or 90 days, depending on the nature of the issues and company policy.

## 4. Provide Support and Resources
Offer coaching, training, or mentoring to help the employee improve. This might include:
- Regular check-ins
- Access to learning materials
- Pairing with a more experienced colleague

## 5. Regular Feedback and Monitoring
Schedule frequent meetings to discuss progress, address challenges, and adjust the plan if necessary.

## 6. Document Everything
Keep detailed records of meetings, feedback, and progress to ensure transparency and fairness.

## 7. Final Review and Decision
At the end of the PIP, evaluate whether the employee has met the improvement goals:
- If yes, acknowledge the progress and continue monitoring.
- If not, consider next steps, which could include reassignment, demotion, or termination.

---

## Example PIP Plan for an Employee
- **Performance Issue:** Missed deadlines on key deliverables for 3 consecutive months.
- **Goals:** Submit all project deliverables on or before deadlines for the next 60 days.
- **Support:** Weekly one-on-one coaching sessions; time management training.
- **Timeline:** 60 days.
- **Monitoring:** Weekly progress reviews.
- **Outcome:** Improvement expected by end of 60 days, with documented evidence.

# Performance Improvement Plan (PIP) - Example

## Employee: John Doe  
## Position: Software Developer  
## Manager: Jane Smith  
## PIP Start Date: January 1, 2026  
## PIP End Date: March 1, 2026  
## Duration: 60 days

---

## 1. Performance Issues Identified
- Consistently missed project deadlines for the past 3 months.
- Code quality issues resulting in increased bugs and rework.
- Limited participation in team meetings and collaboration.

## 2. Improvement Goals
- Submit all assigned project deliverables on or before deadlines for the next 60 days.
- Reduce code defects by 50% through improved testing and code reviews.
- Actively participate in at least 80% of team meetings and contribute ideas.

## 3. Support and Resources Provided
- Weekly one-on-one coaching sessions with the manager.
- Access to time management and coding best practices training.
- Pair programming sessions with a senior developer twice a week.

## 4. Monitoring and Feedback
- Weekly progress review meetings to discuss deliverables and challenges.
- Code review feedback provided within 24 hours.
- Manager and peer feedback collected mid-way through the PIP.

---

## 5. Outcome and Final Review

### Summary of Progress:
- John consistently met all project deadlines during the PIP period.
- Code defects reduced by approximately 60%, exceeding the goal.
- Actively participated in team meetings and contributed valuable suggestions.
- Demonstrated improved communication and collaboration skills.

### Manager's Comments:
John showed a strong commitment to improving his performance. The coaching sessions and training were effective, and John took initiative to seek help when needed. His turnaround has positively impacted the team's productivity.

### Conclusion:
John has successfully met the goals outlined in the PIP and is removed from the Performance Improvement Plan as of March 1, 2026. Continued monitoring will ensure sustained performance.
