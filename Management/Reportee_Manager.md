# Bare Minimum Expectations for Your Reporting Managers

As a senior manager, here are the **non-negotiable baseline expectations** from your direct reports who are managers:

---

## 1. Delivery & Results

- **Hit agreed-upon goals** and OKRs consistently
- Deliver **on time** with acceptable quality
- Take **ownership** of outcomes (no excuses)
- Manage **scope and timelines** realistically

### Example
❌ Missed deadline with no warning ✅ Delivered on time or flagged risks 2 weeks prior

---

## 2. Team Performance & Health

- Maintain **team morale and retention**
- Conduct **regular 1-on-1s** with direct reports (non-negotiable)
- Provide **clear feedback and development**
- Identify and develop **high performers**

### Expectations
- Weekly 1-on-1s with each direct report
- Annual performance reviews on time
- Exit interviews for departing employees

---

## 3. Communication & Transparency

- **Escalate issues early** (not at crisis point)
- Provide **weekly/bi-weekly status updates**
- No **surprises** in team metrics or problems
- Share **bad news immediately**

---

## 4. Accountability & Integrity

- **Own mistakes** and learn from them
- Be **honest** about capabilities and timelines
- Don't **blame team members** to avoid responsibility
- Speak **truth to power** when needed

---

## 5. Alignment with Company Values

- Model **company culture and values**
- Treat **all employees fairly**
- Follow **compliance and policies**
- Advocate for **diverse and inclusive teams**

---

## 6. Problem-Solving

- **Solve 80% of problems** at their level
- Only escalate when truly needed
- Come with **solutions**, not just problems
- Show **critical thinking**

### Example
❌ "My team is struggling" ✅ "My team is struggling. Here's what I've tried, here's what's working, and here's where I need help"

---

## 7. Development & Growth Mindset

- Take **ownership of their own development**
- Ask for **feedback** actively
- Show willingness to **learn and adapt**
- Stay **current** with industry trends (in tech, this is critical)

---

## 8. Budget & Resource Management

- Manage **budget responsibly**
- No **wasteful spending**
- Justify **resource requests** with business case
- Track and report **spend accurately**

---

## 9. Collaboration

- Work **cross-functionally** without silos
- Support **peer managers** and company goals
- Avoid **turf wars or politics**
- Share **knowledge and best practices**

---

## 10. Responsiveness

- **Respond to communication** in reasonable time
- Attend **required meetings**
- Be **accessible** to their team
- Honor **commitments and deadlines**

---

## The "Three Strikes" Rule

Consider this framework for accountability:

| Strike | Action |
|--------|--------|
| **1st** | Clear conversation, written expectations, support |
| **2nd** | Documented PIP or performance plan |
| **3rd** | Career transition discussion |

---

## Red Flags - Zero Tolerance

These are **instant problems** requiring immediate action:
🚨 Integrity issues (dishonesty, data manipulation) 
🚨 Harassment or discrimination 
🚨 Complete project failures with no warning 
🚨 Losing key talent due to poor management 
🚨 Regulatory or compliance violations 
🚨 Abusive behavior toward team members

---

## What You Should NOT Expect

❌ Perfection or 100% success rate 
❌ That they never make mistakes 
❌ Working 60+ hour weeks consistently 
❌ No questions or disagreement 
❌ Handling everything alone (that's what managers do)

---

## How to Communicate These Expectations

In your first meeting with a reporting manager:

```markdown
"Here's what I expect from you as a manager reporting to me:

1. Own your results completely
2. Keep me informed with no surprises
3. Develop your team intentionally
4. Be honest and transparent
5. Solve problems before escalating
6. Model our company values daily
```

I will support you, provide resources, and have your back.
In return, I need you to meet these baseline standards.
Does this make sense? Do you have questions?"

---
### Support You Should Provide
To make these expectations realistic:

- Executive coaching for new managers
- Clear communication of priorities
- Resources and budget for team success
- Removal of blockers you can remove
- Regular feedback (not just annual reviews)
- Psychological safety to take risks

---
### Summary Checklist
- Clear expectations documented
- Regular feedback cadence established
- Support systems in place
- Performance metrics defined
- Accountability framework agreed upon
- Career development plan discussed
- Communication channels clear
- Escalation criteria defined
